# Why Is My Electric Shower Not Working? (Complete Fix Guide)

An electric shower not working is a common household issue, especially in older installations or during colder months. In most cases, the problem is not a broken unit, but a **power supply issue, water flow restriction, or internal safety cut-out**.

This guide will help you **diagnose the exact cause step by step and safely restore your shower in most cases without a technician**.

---

## 📷 1. How an electric shower works

![Electric shower system overview](/images/electric-shower-not-working/shower-1-overview.jpg "Diagram showing electric shower heating element and water flow system")

An electric shower heats cold water instantly using an internal heating element.

Basic process:
- Cold water enters shower unit
- Water flows through heating element
- Temperature is controlled electronically
- Heated water exits through shower head

If either **electricity or water flow is disrupted**, the shower will stop working properly.

---

## ⚡ 2. No power to the shower (most common issue)

![Electric shower control unit no power](/images/electric-shower-not-working/shower-2-power.jpg "Electric shower control panel showing no lights or power")

### Symptoms
- No lights on shower unit
- No response when turning on
- Completely dead system

### Why it happens
- Tripped fuse or breaker
- Isolator switch turned off
- Faulty wiring or connection issue

### Fix
- Check consumer unit (fuse box)
- Reset tripped breaker
- Ensure shower isolator switch is ON
- Check if other appliances on same circuit work

---

## 💧 3. Low water flow (very common hidden cause)

![Low water flow electric shower issue](/images/electric-shower-not-working/shower-3-water-flow.jpg "Weak water stream coming from electric shower head")

### Symptoms
- Shower turns on but water is weak or stops
- Unit switches off during use
- Temperature fluctuates

### Why it happens
- Blocked shower head
- Kinked hose
- Low household water pressure
- Inlet filter clogged

### Fix
- Remove and clean shower head
- Check hose for bends or restrictions
- Clean inlet filter screen
- Ensure main water valve is fully open

---

## 🔥 4. Overheating safety cut-out (protective shutdown)

![Electric shower overheating warning system](/images/electric-shower-not-working/shower-4-overheat.jpg "Electric shower internal safety cut-out mechanism")

### Symptoms
- Shower starts then stops
- Water suddenly goes cold
- Unit needs time before restarting

### Why it happens
- Blocked water flow
- Excessive heat buildup inside unit
- Mineral scale on heating element

### Fix
- Improve water flow (see section above)
- Turn shower off and let it cool down
- Descale shower head and internal filter
- Restart after a few minutes

---

## 🌡️ 5. Temperature control or internal fault

![Electric shower temperature control dial](/images/electric-shower-not-working/shower-5-temperature.jpg "Shower temperature control knob and internal heating system")

### Symptoms
- Water stays cold or lukewarm
- Temperature knob has no effect
- Inconsistent heating

### Why it happens
- Faulty thermostat
- Heating element wear
- Internal electrical fault

### Fix
- Reset unit if possible
- Try different temperature settings
- If no change → likely internal failure requiring technician

---

## 🧠 Quick diagnosis checklist (SEO snippet optimized)

If your electric shower is not working:

- No lights → power issue
- Weak water → flow restriction
- Starts then stops → overheating protection
- No heat → thermostat or element fault
- Intermittent issues → scale buildup

---

## ⚡ Step-by-step full fix checklist

- Check fuse box and isolator switch
- Test if unit has power
- Clean shower head and hose
- Check water inlet flow
- Allow unit to cool if overheated
- Reset shower system if possible

## ❓ People also ask (SEO FAQ section)

### Why has my electric shower stopped working suddenly?
Most commonly due to tripped fuse, overheating protection, or low water flow.

### Why is my electric shower cold?
This can be caused by a faulty heating element or insufficient water pressure.

### Why does my electric shower keep cutting out?
Usually due to overheating caused by restricted water flow.

### Can I reset an electric shower?
Yes, most units have a built-in safety reset that activates after cooling down.

---

## ⚠️ When to call a professional

Contact a qualified electrician or plumber if:

- No power after checking fuse box
- Burning smell or electrical noise
- Persistent overheating shutdowns
- Suspected internal heating element failure
- Water leaks from unit casing
