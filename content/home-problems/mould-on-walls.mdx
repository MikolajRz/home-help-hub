# Mould on Walls: Causes, Removal & Prevention Guide

Mould on walls is one of the most common household problems, especially in older or poorly ventilated homes. While it may look like a surface issue, mould is usually a sign of a **deeper moisture or ventilation problem**.

This guide explains how to identify the type of mould, remove it safely, and prevent it from returning permanently.

---

## ⚠️ 1. What type of mould are you dealing with?

![Black mould on wall corner](/images/mould-on-walls/mould-1-black.jpg "Black mould growing in wall corner due to moisture")

### Common types
- **Black mould (Stachybotrys)** – most serious, often toxic in high exposure
- **Green mould** – usually from damp plaster or condensation
- **White mould** – early-stage growth on walls or cold surfaces

### Signs of mould
- Black or green patches on walls or ceilings
- Musty smell in the room
- Peeling paint or wallpaper
- Cold, damp wall surfaces

👉 Identifying mould type helps determine severity, but **cause is always moisture**.

---

## 💧 2. Condensation (most common cause)

![Condensation on cold wall surface](/images/mould-on-walls/mould-2-condensation.jpg "Water droplets forming on cold interior wall surface")

### Why it happens
- Warm air hitting cold walls
- Poor ventilation in rooms
- High indoor humidity (cooking, showering, drying clothes)

### Where it appears
- Corners of rooms
- Behind furniture
- Near windows
- Bathroom ceilings and walls

### Fix
- Improve ventilation (open windows daily)
- Use extractor fans in bathrooms/kitchens
- Keep furniture slightly away from walls
- Use a dehumidifier in humid rooms

---

## 🧱 3. Cold bridges (structural cause)

![Cold bridge mould in wall corner](/images/mould-on-walls/mould-3-coldbridge.jpg "Mould forming on thermal bridge inside building structure")

### What is it?
A cold bridge is a section of wall where heat escapes faster, causing condensation.

### Symptoms
- Mould always appears in the same spot
- Often in corners or ceiling edges
- Worse in winter months

### Fix
- Improve insulation
- Install thermal wall lining
- Reduce indoor humidity
- Use anti-condensation paint (temporary solution)

---

## 🚿 4. Water leaks inside walls

![Hidden pipe leak causing damp wall](/images/mould-on-walls/mould-4-leak.jpg "Hidden plumbing leak inside wall leading to mould growth")

### Symptoms
- Localized damp patch
- Wall feels soft or swollen
- Mould keeps returning after cleaning
- Possible water meter movement when no water is used

### Fix
- Check plumbing behind affected wall
- Inspect pipes in bathrooms/kitchens
- Repair leaking joints or pipes
- Dry wall completely before repainting

---

## 🌧️ 5. Rising damp (older homes issue)

![Rising damp on lower wall section](/images/mould-on-walls/mould-5-risingdamp.jpg "Moisture rising from ground into wall structure")

### Symptoms
- Damp patches starting from floor level
- White salt deposits (efflorescence)
- Peeling paint near skirting boards

### Fix
- Install or repair damp-proof course (DPC)
- Improve external drainage
- Inject chemical damp proofing (professional solution)

---

## 🧼 6. How to safely remove mould

![Cleaning mould from wall surface](/images/mould-on-walls/mould-6-cleaning.jpg "Person cleaning mould from wall using cleaning solution")

### Step-by-step cleaning

- Wear gloves and mask (important)
- Ventilate the room
- Apply mould remover or vinegar solution
- Scrub gently with sponge or brush
- Dry the wall completely

💡 Important: Cleaning without fixing moisture = mould returns.

---

## ⚠️ What NOT to do

- Do NOT paint over mould
- Do NOT ignore small patches
- Do NOT dry brush without protection (spores spread)
- Do NOT skip ventilation improvements

---

## 🔍 How to identify the real cause (quick checklist)

- Mould near window? → condensation
- Mould in corners? → cold bridge
- Mould near plumbing wall? → leak
- Mould near floor? → rising damp

---

## 🛠️ Prevention tips

- Keep humidity below 60%
- Ventilate daily (10–20 minutes)
- Use extractor fans consistently
- Avoid drying clothes indoors
- Insulate cold walls
- Move furniture away from exterior walls

---

## ❓ People also ask (SEO FAQ section)

### Is mould on walls dangerous?
Yes, long-term exposure can cause allergies, breathing issues, and irritation.

### Why does mould keep coming back after cleaning?
Because the moisture source (condensation, leak, or cold bridge) is not fixed.

### Can I remove mould permanently?
Yes, but only if the underlying moisture problem is solved.

---

## 📌 Summary

Mould on walls is caused by:
- condensation from poor ventilation
- cold structural bridges
- hidden water leaks
- rising damp in older buildings

The key is not just cleaning mould — but eliminating the **moisture source that feeds it**.
