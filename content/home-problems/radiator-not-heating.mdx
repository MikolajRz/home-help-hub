# Why Is My Radiator Not Heating Properly?

A radiator not heating properly is one of the most common heating system issues in homes. In most cases, the issue is not a broken radiator, but a simple circulation problem such as trapped air, low boiler pressure, or a partially closed valve.

This guide will help you **diagnose the exact cause and fix it step by step**, even without technical experience.

---

## 📷 1. How a radiator heating system works

![Central heating system overview](/images/radiator-not-heating/radiator-1-overview.jpg "Central heating system diagram showing water circulation through radiators")

A central heating system works by circulating hot water from the boiler through pipes and radiators. If circulation is interrupted, heat distribution becomes uneven or stops completely.

---

## 🫧 2. Air trapped inside the radiator (most common issue)

![Air trapped inside radiator](/images/radiator-not-heating/radiator-2-air-trapped.jpg "Air trapped in radiator causing cold top section")

### Symptoms:
- Radiator is cold at the top
- Bottom is still warm
- Gurgling or bubbling noises inside the radiator

### Why it happens:
- Air naturally builds up in the system over time
- After refilling the heating system
- After maintenance or pressure drops

### Fix (bleeding the radiator):
- Turn off the heating system and wait for it to cool
- Place a cloth or towel under the bleed valve
- Insert a radiator key into the valve
- Slowly open the valve until air starts escaping
- Wait until water starts flowing steadily
- Close the valve tightly
- Turn heating back on and check pressure

---

## 🔧 3. Low boiler pressure

![Boiler pressure gauge showing system pressure](/images/radiator-not-heating/radiator-5-pressure-boiler.jpg "Boiler pressure gauge indicating low heating system pressure")

### Symptoms:
- Multiple radiators not heating properly
- Boiler warning or error indicator
- Heating system switches off unexpectedly

### Ideal pressure:
- Normal range: **1.0 – 1.5 bar**

### Fix:
- Check pressure gauge on boiler
- If below 1.0 bar, refill system using filling loop
- Increase pressure to recommended range
- Restart heating system

---

## 🚿 4. Closed or partially closed radiator valve

![Radiator valve adjustment](/images/radiator-not-heating/radiator-3-valve.jpg "Thermostatic radiator valve controlling water flow")

### Symptoms:
- One radiator completely cold
- Other radiators working normally

### Fix:
- Check thermostatic radiator valve (TRV)
- Make sure it is not set to minimum
- Fully open lockshield valve if required
- Ensure valve is not stuck or blocked

---

## 🧱 5. Sludge or internal blockage (advanced issue)

![Sludge buildup inside radiator](/images/radiator-not-heating/radiator-4-bleeding-process.jpg "Radiator system flushing / bleeding process illustration")

### Symptoms:
- Bottom of radiator remains cold
- Radiator heats unevenly
- No improvement after bleeding or pressure fix

### Fix:
- Perform chemical system flush
- Clean or install magnetic filter
- In severe cases, call heating engineer for power flush

---

## ⚖️ 6. System imbalance

Sometimes radiators closer to the boiler receive more hot water than others.

### Fix:
- Adjust lockshield valves across system
- Balance heating distribution
- Ensure even flow between all radiators

---

## 🔧 Full troubleshooting checklist

If your radiator is not heating properly:

- Check boiler pressure
- Bleed radiator
- Check radiator valves
- Test other radiators
- Look for sludge or blockage signs
- Balance heating system if needed

---

## ⚠️ When to call a professional

Contact a heating engineer if:

- Boiler pressure keeps dropping
- Radiators stay cold after bleeding
- Boiler shows persistent error codes
- You suspect internal blockage or corrosion
