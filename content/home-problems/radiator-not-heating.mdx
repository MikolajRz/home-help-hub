# Why Is My Radiator Not Heating Properly?

A radiator not heating properly is one of the most common heating system issues in homes. In most cases, the issue is not a broken radiator, but a simple circulation problem such as trapped air, low boiler pressure, or a partially closed valve.

This guide will help you **diagnose the exact cause and fix it step by step**, even without technical experience.

---

## 📷 1. How a radiator heating system works

![Central heating system overview](/images/radiator-not-heating/radiator-1-overview.jpg "Central heating system diagram showing water circulation through radiators")

A central heating system works by circulating hot water from the boiler through pipes and radiators. If circulation is interrupted, heat distribution becomes uneven or stops completely.

---

## 🫧 2. Air trapped inside the radiator (most common issue)

![Air trapped inside radiator](/images/radiator-not-heating/radiator-2-air-trapped.jpg "Air trapped in radiator causing cold top section")

### Symptoms:
- Radiator is cold at the top but warm at the bottom
- Gurgling or bubbling noises
- Uneven heat distribution

### Why it happens:
Air enters the system naturally over time, especially after refilling or maintenance.

### Fix (bleeding the radiator):
1. Turn off the heating system
2. Place a cloth under the bleed valve
3. Insert radiator key and slowly open valve
4. Wait until air stops and water starts flowing
5. Close valve tightly
6. Check boiler pressure afterwards

---

## 🔧 3. Low boiler pressure

![Boiler pressure gauge showing low pressure](/images/radiator-not-heating/radiator-3-pressure.jpg "Boiler pressure gauge indicating low system pressure")

### Symptoms:
- Multiple radiators not heating
- Boiler warning or error light
- Heating system shuts down intermittently

### Ideal pressure:
Most systems operate between **1.0 and 1.5 bar**

### Fix:
1. Check boiler pressure gauge
2. If below 1.0 bar, refill using filling loop
3. Restore pressure to recommended level
4. Restart heating system

---

## 🚿 4. Closed or partially closed radiator valve

![Radiator valve adjustment](/images/radiator-not-heating/radiator-4-valve.jpg "Thermostatic radiator valve controlling water flow")

### Symptoms:
- One radiator not heating at all
- Other radiators work normally

### Fix:
- Check thermostatic valve is not set too low
- Fully open lockshield valve if needed
- Ensure valve is not stuck or blocked

---

## 🧱 5. Sludge or internal blockage (advanced issue)

![Blocked radiator due to sludge buildup](/images/radiator-not-heating/radiator-5-sludge.jpg "Radiator with internal sludge causing poor heat circulation")

### Symptoms:
- Bottom of radiator remains cold
- Slow or uneven heating
- No improvement after bleeding

### Solution:
- System flush (chemical or power flush)
- Magnetic filter cleaning
- Professional heating engineer if severe

---

## ⚖️ 6. System imbalance (hidden cause)

Sometimes radiators near the boiler take most of the heat, leaving others cold.

### Fix:
- Adjust lockshield valves
- Balance flow across all radiators
- Ensure even heat distribution across system

---

## 🔧 Full step-by-step checklist

If your radiator is not heating:

1. Check boiler pressure
2. Bleed radiator
3. Check valves
4. Test other radiators
5. Check for sludge symptoms
6. Balance heating system

---

## ⚠️ When to call a professional

Contact a heating engineer if:
- Pressure keeps dropping
- Radiators remain cold after bleeding
- Boiler shows persistent errors
- You suspect internal blockage
