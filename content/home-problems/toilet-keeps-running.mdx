# Toilet Keeps Running: Causes, Fixes & Water Waste Guide

A toilet that keeps running after flushing is one of the most common plumbing problems in homes. While it may seem minor, it can waste a significant amount of water and increase utility bills.

In most cases, the issue is caused by a **fault inside the cistern mechanism**, not a broken toilet.

This guide explains all causes, how to diagnose them, and how to fix the problem step by step.

---

## ⚠️ 1. How to recognize a running toilet

![Toilet cistern water running continuously](/images/toilet-keeps-running/toilet-1-running.jpg "Water continuously flowing inside toilet cistern")

### Common symptoms
- Constant sound of running water
- Toilet refills every few minutes
- Water trickling into bowl
- Higher than normal water bill

👉 Even a small leak can waste hundreds of liters per day.

---

## 🧱 2. Faulty flapper valve (most common cause)

![Toilet flapper valve inside cistern](/images/toilet-keeps-running/toilet-2-flapper.jpg "Rubber flapper valve not sealing properly inside toilet cistern")

### What it is
The flapper is a rubber seal that controls water release from the cistern into the toilet bowl.

### Why it fails
- Rubber wears out over time
- Misalignment after flushing
- Mineral buildup prevents sealing

### Symptoms
- Water constantly leaking into bowl
- Toilet flush weak or inconsistent
- Problem stops briefly when lid is lifted

### Fix
- Inspect flapper for damage
- Clean sealing surface
- Replace flapper if worn (cheap part)
- Ensure correct alignment

---

## 🚰 3. Fill valve malfunction

![Toilet fill valve mechanism](/images/toilet-keeps-running/toilet-3-fillvalve.jpg "Fill valve controlling water refill inside toilet cistern")

### What it does
Controls water refilling after flushing.

### Symptoms
- Water never stops filling
- Overflow pipe running continuously
- Loud hissing noise

### Fix
- Adjust float height
- Clean inlet valve from debris
- Replace fill valve if faulty
- Check water pressure regulator

---

## ⚖️ 4. Incorrect float level

![Toilet float adjustment mechanism](/images/toilet-keeps-running/toilet-4-float.jpg "Float controlling water level inside toilet cistern")

### Why it matters
If float is set too high, water constantly spills into overflow tube.

### Symptoms
- Water runs into overflow pipe
- Cistern overfills repeatedly

### Fix
- Lower float arm or screw adjustment
- Set water level below overflow tube
- Test flush after adjustment

---

## 🧼 5. Mineral buildup or debris

![Limescale buildup in toilet mechanism](/images/toilet-keeps-running/toilet-5-limescale.jpg "Limescale and debris affecting toilet cistern components")

### Why it happens
- Hard water deposits build up over time
- Debris blocks valve sealing surfaces

### Symptoms
- Intermittent running
- Valve not closing smoothly
- No visible damage

### Fix
- Clean internal components
- Use vinegar to dissolve limescale
- Replace worn seals if needed

---

## 🚿 6. Overflow tube issue

![Toilet overflow tube water level problem](/images/toilet-keeps-running/toilet-6-overflow.jpg "Water flowing into overflow pipe due to incorrect cistern level")

### Symptoms
- Continuous water flow into bowl
- Fill valve never fully stops

### Fix
- Adjust water level lower
- Check fill valve shut-off point
- Replace faulty mechanism if needed

---

## 🔎 Step-by-step diagnosis checklist

Follow this order:

1. Check if water is going into bowl → flapper issue
2. Check if cistern keeps filling → fill valve issue
3. Check overflow pipe → float level issue
4. Check for buildup → cleaning required

---

## ⚠️ What NOT to do

- Do NOT ignore running water (wastes huge amounts)
- Do NOT force internal parts
- Do NOT overtighten plastic fittings
- Do NOT delay replacing worn seals

---

## 🛠️ Prevention tips

- Replace flapper every 3–5 years
- Clean cistern annually
- Check float level periodically
- Remove limescale buildup regularly
- Monitor water bill for unusual increases

## 📌 Summary

A toilet that keeps running is usually caused by:
- faulty flapper valve
- fill valve malfunction
- incorrect float level

Most cases are easy DIY fixes and require only basic adjustments or cheap replacement parts.
