# Window Draught (Cold Air Leaks): Causes, Fixes & Insulation Guide

A draughty window is a common household problem that leads to **heat loss, higher energy bills, and uncomfortable cold spots** in rooms. In most cases, the issue is caused by worn seals, poor insulation, or small gaps in the window frame rather than a defective window.

This guide explains how to find the source of the draught and fix it effectively.

---

## ❄️ 1. How to recognize a window draught

![Cold air leaking around window frame](/images/window-draught/window-1-leak.jpg "Cold air entering through gaps around window frame")

### Common symptoms
- Cold air felt near windows
- Curtains moving slightly without open window
- Higher heating bills in winter
- Condensation around edges
- Uneven room temperature

👉 Draughts often worsen in windy weather.

---

## 🪟 2. Worn or damaged window seals (most common cause)

![Rubber window seal worn out](/images/window-draught/window-2-seal.jpg "Old cracked rubber window gasket causing air leaks")

### Why it happens
- Rubber seals degrade over time
- Exposure to sunlight and temperature changes
- Poor-quality original installation

### Symptoms
- Air leaks around edges of closed window
- Visible cracks in rubber gasket
- Noise from outside more noticeable

### Fix
- Inspect all window seals
- Replace damaged rubber gaskets
- Use self-adhesive weather stripping
- Press seals firmly into frame

💡 This is the most effective long-term fix.

---

## 🧱 3. Gaps in window frame or wall junction

![Gap between window frame and wall](/images/window-draught/window-3-gap.jpg "Visible gap between window frame and wall causing draught")

### Why it happens
- Poor installation work
- Building movement over time
- Shrinking foam insulation

### Symptoms
- Strong cold air in corners
- Draught even with sealed window closed
- Visible cracks around frame

### Fix
- Apply expanding foam (for larger gaps)
- Use silicone sealant for small cracks
- Repaint and reseal after drying
- Consider professional resealing for large gaps

---

## 🪵 4. Poor glazing or old single-pane windows

![Old single glazed window condensation](/images/window-draught/window-4-glazing.jpg "Single pane window causing heat loss and condensation")

### Why it happens
- Old single-glazed windows
- Lack of thermal insulation layer
- Thin glass allows heat transfer

### Symptoms
- Cold glass surface in winter
- Constant condensation
- Room loses heat quickly

### Fix
- Upgrade to double or triple glazing
- Install thermal window film
- Use thick thermal curtains
- Add secondary glazing panels

---

## 🧼 5. Improper window closing or alignment

![Misaligned window latch mechanism](/images/window-draught/window-5-latch.jpg "Window latch not sealing properly causing air leakage")

### Why it happens
- Misaligned hinges or frame shift
- Loose or worn locking mechanism

### Symptoms
- Window closes but not tightly sealed
- One side feels looser than the other
- Audible air movement near latch

### Fix
- Adjust window hinges
- Tighten locking mechanisms
- Replace worn hardware if needed

---

## 🧵 6. Temporary sealing issues (quick fixes)

![Temporary window insulation film](/images/window-draught/window-6-film.jpg "Plastic insulation film applied to window for winter protection")

### Solutions
- Draft excluder tape
- Thermal insulation film
- Heavy curtains
- Draft stoppers at window base

👉 These are short-term but effective seasonal fixes.

---

## 🔎 Step-by-step diagnosis checklist

Follow this order:

1. Feel for air leaks around edges
2. Check rubber seals condition
3. Inspect frame-wall junctions
4. Test window locking tightness
5. Evaluate glazing type

---

## ⚠️ What NOT to do

- Do NOT ignore small draughts (they worsen over time)
- Do NOT apply sealant without cleaning surfaces first
- Do NOT block ventilation completely (moisture risk)
- Do NOT over-tighten window mechanisms

---

## 🛠️ Prevention tips

- Check seals every autumn
- Clean and maintain window frames
- Reapply sealant when cracks appear
- Upgrade insulation before winter season
- Avoid excessive humidity indoors

## ❓ People also ask (SEO FAQ section)

### Why is cold air coming from my closed window?
Usually due to worn seals or small gaps in the frame.

### Can you fix draughty windows without replacing them?
Yes, most issues can be fixed with sealing or insulation upgrades.

### Are draughty windows expensive to fix?
Minor fixes are cheap, full replacement is more costly but long-term efficient.

---

## 📌 Summary

Window draughts are usually caused by:
- worn rubber seals
- gaps in frame or wall junction
- old single-glazed windows
- misaligned locking systems

Most draught problems can be significantly reduced with sealing, insulation film, or simple hardware adjustments.
